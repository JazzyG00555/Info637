APPLE HEALTH MENSTRUAL HEALTH APPLICATION REDESIGN

Overview

How might we redesign the Apple Health application for adult working women to be informed about their fertility cycles?

We aim to deliver an application that provides better and more diverse access to fertility information since this is a topic many women would benefit from in their daily lives.

Duration: 5 months

Team Members: Nicole Perez and Azka Qasim

Contributions: Competitive Analysis, User Research, UX Design, Usability Testing

Design Considerations

1. Efficient Tracking Method

A more labour-saving tracking method for logging cycle information. This creates more accessibility and time efficiency.

2. Privacy and Security

Mindfulness towards the lack of current laws protecting those with a uterus, and maintain full transparency with users about what happens to their data.

3. Health and Medical Accuracy

Prioritizing reliable educational content that informs users about their cycle, symptoms and fertility predictions accurately.

Research

Primary

  • Inconsistent Tracking: Users tracked their menstrual health by using apps, manual notes or would forget to track it leading to accuracy issues.
  • Healthcare Barriers: Lack of available accurate medical information. Participants felt that menstrual health was not adequately addressed in medical settings.
  • Privacy Concerns with Tracking Apps: Participants were hesitant with sharing their data on apps due to data security and third party sharing.
  • Low Ovulation Tracking Awareness: Ovulation tacking was less common. Some participants expressed confusion about how ovulation tracking works.
  • Gaps in Education: Participants felt they lacked clear, reliable information about their cycles, often learning through trial and error or informal sources.

Secondary

  • How Data is shared in Apple Health: Apple encrypts all information from their health apps except for the Medical ID. They won’t share one’s data unless it was requested by the user.
  • Medical Data can be Shared Legally: HIPAA, can't stop data from applications, such as menstrual health apps, from being accessed. Law enforcement can access this information anytime as there are no laws against doing this currently.
  • Equitable knowledge of Menstrual health and Fertility for all: New York State has a legislative package called Health Equity and Opportunity which requires women to have equitable access to resources when regarding their reproductive health. Menstrual equity revolves around, not only providing equal access to menstrual products, but also around having an equitable and unstigmatized education about reproductive health. More accessibility to fertility and menstrual health information allows for women to have a better understanding of their overall body.

Feature Developments

  • A more detailed calendar view, offering users an at-a-glance summary of their cycle, symptoms, and ovulation predictions.
  • A dedicated Fertility Tracking page, with tools that log ovulation, pregnancy, fertility symptoms, and any related habits such as IVF tracking or temperature charting.
  • A new Resources section, that gives users access to medically-sound articles, tips related to fertility health and a section that informs users of how their data is being used.

Future Scope

1. More Customizable Notifications for Tracking

A more developed notification system to allow users to track their fertility cycles more efficiently. This would include customizable notifications for IVF injection tracking, different contraception tracking depending on the type of contraception or even more specific notifications for ovulation tracking. Providing users with the ability to choose the type of notifications they need to track their fertility is a feature to be further tested.

2. More Developed Mental Health Checks and Sections

Different and more detailed mental health checks that could suggest reasons as to why a user may be feeling a certain way. These mental health checks could be further researched and developed with psychologists or therapists in the field before being implemented within the application.

3. Medical Locations Suggestions Based on Insurance

More developed and thorough suggestions could be generated with a section allowing users to upload their insurance options and what they can medically afford. Therefore, when the application suggests medical locations nearby, it could easily suggest ones the user can afford. It could also potentially let users know if there are medical locations nearby that do not match their insurance as well.

4. Discussion Boards

A place for users to discuss their questions with medical professionals and other users. This could be a place to allow users to share experiences together, such as traumatic birth experiences, or tips on how to balance work and a new family. It would also allow adult working women to ask medical professionals questions if they have a hard time fitting in a medical appointment in their busy schedules.

5. Discrete Privacy Mode

A discrete privacy mode screen design would be a future implementation for women who do not want to reveal they are using a menstruation application at work. People are usually private with their medical data and not every adult working woman wants to let the office know if they have an IUD or if they are trying for a child.

Reflections

What Worked Well

  • Implementing Summary and Log tabs created a clear and easy-to-read layout.
  • Could visually see users’ clicking patterns which allowed us to receive valuable feedback to further adjust our redesign.

What Did Not Work Well

  • Our observations did not match our design frame needs. Being within a medical space limited our ability to gauge behaviors since we could not enter waiting room(s).
  • Users did not understand the subsections of the Health Resources Page. This seemed to be due to the vocabulary and how the options were named within our prototype.
